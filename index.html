About the Chapter

Advanced systems, clean execution.

The Purdue AESS chapter is a multidisciplinary engineering team focused on the design, integration, and validation of advanced flight systems. Members gain hands-on experience across aerodynamics, propulsion, structures, controls, and systems engineering by working on real hardware and high-fidelity analysis. The team emphasizes disciplined engineering, practical problem-solving, and building systems that extend beyond typical collegiate projects.

Flagship Project

SSUAV Program

The Supersonic Small Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (SSUAV) program focuses on the design, build, and validation of a sub-25 kg air-breathing platform capable of exceeding Mach 1 under strict performance and system constraints.

Design Philosophy

The aircraft is built around a defined mission profile: carrier aircraft launch, release, acceleration to Mach 1, pull-up, and parachute recovery.

Aerodynamics

Supersonic configuration, low-drag shaping, and stability.

Propulsion

Inlet performance, engine integration, and system matching.

Mass-efficient design and manufacturable composite structure.

Controls

Flight dynamics, guidance, and system validation.

Development

Validation, testing, and build progress.

The program has progressed from analysis to physical validation, including wind tunnel testing, manufacturing development, and subsystem integration. These efforts validate key assumptions, inform design refinement, and reduce risk ahead of full vehicle build and flight.

Wind tunnel testing

A full-scale model was designed, built, and tested in the Boeing Subsonic Wind Tunnel at the Purdue Aerospace Sciences Lab. Testing focused on low-speed stability, control effectiveness, and overall aerodynamic behavior, validating analysis methods and informing configuration refinement.

Structures and fabrication

Structural development focuses on manufacturable, mass-efficient composite construction under aggressive load requirements. Ongoing work includes material selection, layup process development, and fabrication trials to ensure reliable flight hardware.

Subteams

Five technical groups. One integrated aircraft.

The team is organized around core engineering groups that own the vehicle from concept through validation, with leadership and outreach supporting execution, recruiting, and external engagement.

Design / Aerodynamics

Configuration, stability, performance

Owns airframe layout, aerodynamic shaping, drag reduction, stability work, and high-speed performance analysis. This group supports CFD, DATCOM, wind tunnel planning, and design refinement.

Structures

Composite build strategy, validation, manufacturability

Develops the structure around aggressive mass and load targets while evaluating layup methods, reinforcement-to-epoxy ratios, and fabrication processes for reliable flight hardware.

Systems

Avionics, controls, integration

Handles embedded systems, controls architecture, vehicle integration, and the bridge between simulation, onboard hardware, and operational execution.

Propulsion

Engine integration, performance, testing

Focuses on propulsion system selection, integration, and refinement for a COTS-based high-speed platform, with supporting analysis across inlet, engine, and overall vehicle performance.

Launch

Carrier aircraft, deployment systems

Responsible for the carrier aircraft and all launch systems, including integration, release mechanisms, and operational execution. This group ensures reliable deployment and mission initiation.
